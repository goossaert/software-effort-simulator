# Cross-quarter Team Projections tab with quick projection Monte Carlo

The **Team Projections tab** (`#tab-projections`, the third **Tab**) is a *cross-quarter*, *per-team* view of the **Initiatives CSV** and **Constant Work CSV**: a single summary table of initiative counts per **Team** per **Quarter**, followed by one **Projection section** per team containing a stacked-bar `Initiatives by Quarter` chart (Must / Should / Could), a paired `Effort Projection by Quarter` bar chart showing the P25 / P50 / P75 of an MSC-scenario **Quick projection Monte Carlo** per quarter, and an **Initiative matrix** listing every in-scope initiative as a row with a per-quarter **MoSCoW** badge column plus footer rows for the per-quarter cumulative count chips and the P50 (P25–P75) effort band. The implementation is `buildTeamProjections` (`index.html:1917-2009`) for the data assembly and `renderTeamProjections` (`index.html:2550-2815`) for the two-level render; both run inside the same **Run** as the org-level and Team Level tabs, against the union of every quarter present in the loaded CSVs — *not* the **Quarter selector**'s **Historical quarter(s)** or **Target quarter(s)** subset.

We chose to give the cross-quarter forecast **its own tab** — rather than nesting it inside the **Team Level tab** or stacking it below the org-level chart — because it answers a different question on a different time axis. The org-level and team-level tabs both forecast *the selected target quarter* by *fitting on the selected historical quarter*; they collapse the time dimension to a single before/after split. The Team Projections tab keeps the time dimension *explicit*: every quarter present in the CSV is a column, every team is a row, and the user can read "Platform commits 8 MSC initiatives in Q2, 5 in Q3, 11 in Q4" without re-running. Folding this into one of the existing tabs would either (a) force the user to flip the **Quarter selector** repeatedly to read across quarters — turning a single planning conversation into a series of Runs — or (b) require a second, parallel quarter-axis widget inside an already-dense tab. A separate tab gives the cross-quarter view the horizontal room it needs (a quarters × teams table) and keeps the other two tabs focused on the selected-quarter forecast.

We chose to **render every quarter present in the CSV**, not just the selected **Historical** + **Target** subset, because the planning conversation this tab supports is *forward-looking past the immediate target*. A team is rarely deciding only "what to commit in Q3" in isolation; they are deciding "what is in Q3 *and* what is in Q4, given what is already booked for Q2". Filtering to the selector subset would amputate exactly the lookahead the tab exists to provide. The cost is that the summary table grows wide on CSVs that span many quarters — handled by `overflow-x: auto` on `#proj-summary-wrap` and `.proj-init-wrap` (`index.html:722`, `770`). The quarter ordering is the chronological sort produced by `extractQuarters(editedInitiatives)` (the same helper that powers the **Quarter selector**), so the time axis reads left-to-right in calendar order.

We chose to compute the per-quarter effort projection with a **Quick projection Monte Carlo** capped at `Math.min(iters, 3000)` iterations (`index.html:3357`) — rather than the full Run's `iters` — because each Projection section runs *one independent Monte Carlo per quarter that has at least one in-scope initiative*. On a CSV with `T` teams × `Q` quarters that is `T × Q` independent simulations *in addition* to the org-level Run and the `T` Team Level Runs. At the default `iters = 10000`, with say 6 teams and 4 quarters, that is already 24 extra Runs inside the same wall-clock budget; at the empty-field fallback `iters = 1000000` it would be 24 million iterations of extra work, locking the main thread for tens of seconds. The 3000 cap is calibrated to the *banding* role the projection plays: the chart only displays the P25, P50, P75 (not the full percentile vector, not `pExceed`), and at 3000 iterations these three quartiles are stable to within roughly 1 PM for typical team-sized distributions — well below the granularity at which a planning conversation reasons. The headline org-level and team-level Runs keep the user-configured `iters` precisely because their **Stats** tables surface `pExceed` and tail percentiles that *do* need 10k–1M iterations to stabilise; the projections do not. The cap is *not* a tail-cutoff (the full lognormal-sum distribution is still sampled, just fewer times) — it is a budget cap. Users who need precise per-quarter percentile bands can read the org-level or team-level tabs for the *selected* target quarter instead.

We chose to **fit the projections with the org-wide λ and bootstrap pool** — passed in verbatim as `orgLambda` and `orgEpicSizingDist` from the run-button handler (`index.html:3358`) — rather than per-team-and-quarter parameters, for two compounding reasons. First, per-team-per-quarter parameters are sample-starved: a single team's historical evidence in a single quarter is often a handful of initiatives, well below the threshold where a fitted λ has any signal-to-noise ([ADR-0019](./0019-per-team-independent-simulations.md) documents the same threshold problem at the team-only level, where the threshold is 4 historical initiatives across the entire selected historical window). A team-quarter cell with `n=1` initiative would generate a meaningless per-cell forecast. Second, the projections view's purpose is *cross-quarter comparison within a team*, not *cross-team comparison of pace*: holding λ and the size pool constant makes each team's row internally comparable across quarters (the only thing varying column-to-column is the K count, which is the planning lever the user is reading). The trade-off is that a team with a genuinely-different pace from the org average sees its bars under- or over-stated relative to the team-level tab's team-scoped Run — surfaced by [ADR-0019](./0019-per-team-independent-simulations.md)'s `Historical data` toggle on the Team Level tab. A future revision could expose the same toggle here; we deliberately did not, because the projections view is already dense and the toggle would mean re-running `T × Q` Monte Carlos per click.

We chose to **fold Constant Work into the projections deterministically** — `cwEffort = sum of cwEpics.effort` is added as `fixedEffort` into `runSimulation` and surfaces as a constant shift on the per-quarter P25/P50/P75 ([feature 0015](../../backtracked-features.md#0015), [ADR-0014](./0014-capacity-and-iterations-as-run-inputs.md)) — and to **render constant-work epics inline in the Initiative matrix**, appended after the MoSCoW-sorted initiatives with a green row tint (`background:#f0fdf4`) and a `[{tshirt} · ~{effort} PM]` annotation in the name cell (`index.html:2627-2634`). The two surfaces tell the same story from different angles: the effort chart shows the *aggregate* PM impact of the constant work on each quarter's projection band; the matrix shows *which specific* constant-work items contribute. Hiding the constant-work rows from the matrix while keeping them in the effort band would silently inflate the effort projection without showing the user why; surfacing the rows but not the band shift would make the matrix legible without explaining the chart. The green tint distinguishes them from sampled initiatives at a glance — green is the same palette cue [feature 0015](../../backtracked-features.md#0015) chose for the constant-work UI elsewhere.

We chose to **show the projection effort as P25 / P50 / P75 bars** — a narrow P50 bar overlaid on a wider semi-transparent P25–P75 range bar — rather than as a histogram, a box-and-whisker, or the same overlapping-Scenarios chart the org tab uses. The projections view is read at a *glance across quarters*; a histogram per quarter would require the user to mentally compare 4–6 separate distributions and the cross-quarter trend would drown in the chart-junk. A simple band-and-median bar reads as a horizon line — the user scans the P50 trend across quarters in one saccade, with the P25–P75 band giving immediate visual weight to the *spread* of risk. We deliberately do not show P10 or P90 because the **Quick projection Monte Carlo**'s 3000-iteration budget makes those points unreliable; surfacing them would invite the user to read precision the engine isn't computing. The "P25 / P50 / P75" three-point band is the same shape as a tukey hinges plot without the outliers — readable without statistical literacy and honest about the resolution.

We chose to **build the team list from every team present anywhere in the CSV** (not just teams in the selected target quarter) because the cross-quarter table is a *full inventory*. A team that has no Q3 commitments but has 8 initiatives in Q4 still belongs in the table — omitting it would silently hide planning work the user explicitly loaded. This is the inverse of the Team Level tab's choice ([ADR-0019](./0019-per-team-independent-simulations.md)), which is bounded by the target-quarter selection precisely because the team-level Run is a *target-quarter forecast*. The same case-insensitive dedup with first-seen casing applies here as on the Team Level tab — the rule is property of the **Team** concept, not of the tab.

We chose to **append constant-work-only quarters to the per-team quarter axis** — `activeQuartersForTeam = [...new Set([...allQuarters, ...cwQuarters])]` (`index.html:1946`) — so that a quarter that has constant-work entries but no Initiatives for the team still appears as a non-empty row in the matrix and contributes a bar to the effort chart. Without this, a team whose entire Q2 commitment is captured as constant work (zero Initiatives, several constant-work epics) would render as an empty column in the projection, contradicting the matrix that lists the rows. The summary table still uses the unaugmented `allQuarters` axis (`index.html:2558-2559`), because the summary table's job is "**Initiatives** per team per quarter" — constant work is *not* an Initiative and should not show up in those counts. The split is intentional: constant work is a parallel input stream, not an alternative representation of an Initiative.

We chose to **sort initiatives in the matrix Must → Should → Could → Won't → Unknown then alphabetically by key** (`index.html:1968-1972`) — rather than by quarter order or insertion order — because the matrix's left-to-right read is "what is the highest-priority commitment first, and how does it map to a quarter". A user looking for "which Musts are in Q3" wants to scan the Must rows at the top and read across; a per-quarter sort would scatter Musts across the matrix and force the user to filter visually. The constant-work rows are appended *after* the MoSCoW sort because they are a distinct category, not a MoSCoW bucket — the green tint already signals their category, and grouping them at the end keeps the prioritised-by-MoSCoW reading order clean.

This decision pairs with [ADR-0006](./0006-monte-carlo-with-bootstrapped-sizes.md) (the **Quick projection Monte Carlo** uses the same sampler as the headline Run, just with a smaller iteration count), [ADR-0010](./0010-three-scenario-moscow-forecasting.md) (only the MSC scenario is used for the projection band — Must Only and Must+Should are not surfaced because the cross-quarter view focuses on the *full committed* effort, not the scenario decomposition), [ADR-0014](./0014-capacity-and-iterations-as-run-inputs.md) (the projection Monte Carlo reads the same Run-level iterations input but caps it at 3000 for budget reasons), [ADR-0015](./0015-capacity-as-auto-managed-chart-marker.md) (the effort projection chart does *not* render a capacity line — there is no per-quarter capacity, and the projection band reads as a forecast not a risk gate), [ADR-0018](./0018-tab-based-results-layout.md) (the Team Projections tab is the third **Tab** and follows the same pre-render-on-Run, reset-to-org-on-Run rules as the others), and [ADR-0019](./0019-per-team-independent-simulations.md) (the Team Projections tab's team list is bounded by *every quarter* in the CSV; the Team Level tab's is bounded by the *target* selection — same dedup rule, different scope). The implementation is `buildTeamProjections` (`index.html:1917-2009`), `renderTeamProjections` (`index.html:2550-2815`), and the run-button handler integration at `index.html:3355-3359`.

A future revision could expose the **Historical data** toggle ([ADR-0019](./0019-per-team-independent-simulations.md)) on the projections tab, letting the user switch between org-wide and team-scoped parameters per Projection section — would re-open this ADR jointly with ADR-0019 because the "always org-wide" rule documented above is the load-bearing decision. A future revision could expose a per-team-per-quarter capacity line on the effort chart — additive, but would re-open [ADR-0015](./0015-capacity-as-auto-managed-chart-marker.md) because the "one auto-managed capacity marker per chart context" rule would need to grow a per-quarter axis. Raising the **Quick projection Monte Carlo** cap above 3000 is a one-line edit that does not re-open this ADR; lowering it below ~1000 would re-open this ADR because the P25/P75 readings would visibly destabilise. Adding a fourth percentile (P10 or P90) to the band would re-open this ADR because the iteration budget would have to grow with the new tail point's precision requirement.
