# Three-scenario MoSCoW forecasting (Must / Must+Should / Must+Should+Could)

> **Status:** superseded by [ADR-0029](./0029-user-defined-groups-supersede-cumulative-moscow.md). The cumulative-three shape no longer survives the generalisation of **MoSCoW** to **Category** ([ADR-0028](./0028-category-as-generalized-moscow.md)) — arbitrary categories have no inherent ordering, so the marginal-cost-of-admitting-the-next-tier framing this ADR defends does not apply. The cumulative semantics is recoverable by the user defining three overlapping **Groups** `{Must}`, `{Must, Should}`, `{Must, Should, Could}`, but it is no longer the engine's contract. The "future revision" paragraph at the foot of this ADR anticipated exactly this change.

Every **Run** executes three side-by-side **Scenarios**, defined by *cumulative* MoSCoW bucket unions over the **Target quarter**'s **Initiatives**: **Must Only** (`K = #Must`), **Must + Should** (`K = #Must + #Should`), and **Must + Should + Could** (`K = #Must + #Should + #Could`). Initiatives whose MoSCoW priority is `Won't` or *unknown* are excluded from every scenario. The three scenarios share the same fitted Poisson λ, the same **Bootstrap pool**, the same capacity, and the same PRNG seed for the duration of the Run; what differs across scenarios is only `K`.

We chose three cumulative scenarios — rather than one (Must only), two (drop Could), or four (split per-bucket) — because the question users actually arrive with is "*if* we commit only to the must-haves vs. *if* we let the team take on everything that's tentatively in scope, how does the risk move?". A cumulative framing answers that directly: P50 and P(effort > capacity) at each step are read as the *marginal* cost of admitting the next priority tier. A per-bucket framing (Must alone, Should alone, Could alone) would force the reader to mentally sum the distributions — and a sum of independent Poisson-lognormal totals is *not* well approximated by the visual sum of three histograms, so the per-bucket view would be statistically misleading for exactly the comparison users want to make. Three scenarios is also the maximum that fits as three coloured columns in the stats table and three overlapping datasets on a single chart without losing legibility; a four-scenario view (e.g. adding "everything including Won't") was not worth the extra column when the Won't bucket is by definition out of scope.

We chose to exclude `Won't` and *unknown* outright — rather than treating them as a fourth scenario, a probabilistic inclusion, or an upper bound — because the simulator's job is to forecast the *committed* plan, not the wishlist. `Won't` is a decision: the team has explicitly de-scoped those initiatives, and including them in any scenario would dishonestly inflate the forecast. *Unknown* is a data-quality signal: a missing or unparseable MoSCoW value means the user has not yet decided where this initiative lives, and the right response is to show that ambiguity in the preview's MoSCoW count breakdown (so they can fix the input) rather than silently fold it into a scenario where the model has no basis to place it. Both are surfaced in the sidebar preview (`moscowGroups.wont`, `moscowGroups.unknown`) so the user can see what is being dropped on this Run.

We chose to *share* the PRNG seed across the three scenarios within a single Run, rather than re-seeding between them, because the three Ks are different views of the same forecast and should not drift apart by an extra Monte Carlo accident on top of their structural difference. Sharing the seed means that whatever epic-count and size-mix realisations get drawn early in the Run flow consistently into all three distributions, and the only thing differing across them is the structural `K`. The seed itself is still re-seeded *once per Run* (`runSimulation` calls `new Xoshiro128ss(...)` and `resetBoxMuller()` at the top — see [ADR-0009](./0009-custom-seeded-prng.md)); back-to-back Runs are not byte-identical by design.

This decision pairs with [ADR-0006](./0006-monte-carlo-with-bootstrapped-sizes.md) (the per-iteration model the three scenarios share), [ADR-0008](./0008-poisson-epic-count.md) (the per-initiative count distribution applied identically across scenarios), and [ADR-0009](./0009-custom-seeded-prng.md) (the once-per-Run seeding discipline this decision relies on). The orchestration lives in `runSimulation` (`index.html:2086`); the MoSCoW bucketing into the three K values lives in `prepareSimulationData` (`index.html:1765-1773`); `normalizeMoscow` (`index.html:1482`) is the single normaliser that makes `Won't` and *unknown* identifiable in both the **Sensible** and **Quirky** CSV formats.

A future revision could expose the bucket set as a user-configurable choice (e.g. let users define their own "minimum / committed / stretch" labels for non-MoSCoW planning conventions) without changing this ADR's *cumulative-three* shape. Moving to a per-bucket view, or to a fourth scenario, would change the shape and would require revisiting this decision.
